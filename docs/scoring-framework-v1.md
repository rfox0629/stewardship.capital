# Scoring Framework v1

## Purpose

The scoring framework turns a fast diagnostic assessment into a useful initial dashboard. It should help users understand their current stewardship stage, identify their top priorities, and know why completing Financial Analysis is the next meaningful step.

Assessment v1 is not financial intake. It should not ask for dollar amounts, uploads, or account connections.

## Locked Scores

Stewardship Capital v1 uses three primary scores:

- Stewardship Score
- Financial Readiness Score
- Legacy Impact Score

Each score should be presented on a simple 0 to 100 scale.

## Locked Stages

The platform maps users into one of five stages:

- Survive
- Stabilize
- Build
- Multiply
- Legacy

Stage language should be practical and encouraging. It should help the user act, not feel judged.

## Locked Pillars

Every recommendation and scored insight should map to one of the four pillars:

- Protect what God has entrusted
- Grow your capacity to serve
- Transfer values and resources to future generations
- Impact Kingdom and community

## Score Definitions

### Stewardship Score

The Stewardship Score is the overall diagnostic score. It reflects clarity, organization, readiness, alignment, and momentum across the user's stewardship life.

Likely inputs:

- Protection readiness
- Growth clarity
- Transfer preparation
- Impact intentionality
- Advisor coordination
- Household or family communication
- Confidence in current planning

### Financial Readiness Score

The Financial Readiness Score reflects whether the household appears ready for deeper planning, analysis, and opportunity identification.

Likely inputs:

- Organized financial picture
- Known professional team
- Confidence in tax, estate, risk, and growth planning
- Awareness of key gaps
- Willingness to complete Financial Analysis

### Legacy Impact Score

The Legacy Impact Score reflects readiness around values transfer, family continuity, generosity, Kingdom impact, and long-term stewardship.

Likely inputs:

- Estate plan awareness
- Family values conversations
- Giving strategy
- Next-generation preparation
- Business or real estate transition awareness
- Community or Kingdom impact intent

## Assessment v1 Constraints

Assessment v1 must follow these requirements:

- Fast diagnostic, not financial intake
- 25 questions max
- No dollar amounts
- No account connections
- No uploads
- Mostly Yes / No / Not Sure and multiple choice
- Auto save shown at top
- Resume later is required
- Designed for 5 to 7 minutes

## Answer Weighting Direction

For Yes / No / Not Sure questions:

- Yes usually indicates readiness, clarity, or completion.
- No usually indicates a gap or opportunity.
- Not Sure indicates uncertainty and should often create a follow-up priority.

For multiple choice questions:

- The clearest, most organized, or most intentional answer receives the highest value.
- The least clear answer receives the lowest value.
- "Not Sure" should reduce confidence and may trigger a dashboard recommendation.

Scoring should not shame the user. A low score is a signal for focus and support.

## Stage Mapping

Recommended initial score bands:

- 0 to 24: Survive
- 25 to 44: Stabilize
- 45 to 64: Build
- 65 to 84: Multiply
- 85 to 100: Legacy

Critical gaps can lower or cap the stage even when other answers are strong. For example, unclear protection, no estate awareness, or no values-transfer conversations may keep a user from being classified as Legacy.

## Stage Meanings

### Survive

The user needs immediate clarity, organization, and protection. Recommendations should focus on simple, stabilizing steps.

### Stabilize

The user has some foundation but still needs better organization and confidence. Recommendations should focus on reducing uncertainty and building a clearer plan.

### Build

The user is ready to coordinate planning, strengthen financial readiness, and explore growth or transfer opportunities.

### Multiply

The user has meaningful complexity or capacity. Recommendations should focus on advanced coordination, tax-aware planning conversations, succession, growth strategy, and impact.

### Legacy

The user is focused on continuity, values transfer, generosity, governance, and multi-generational stewardship.

## Top 3 Priorities

The dashboard should generate the user's top three priorities from:

- Lowest pillar signals
- "No" answers to high-importance questions
- "Not Sure" answers that create uncertainty
- User profile type
- Gaps related to protection, growth, transfer, or impact

Priority language should be plain and action-oriented.

Example priorities:

- Review protection gaps with your professional team.
- Clarify estate and values-transfer plans.
- Complete Financial Analysis to uncover planning opportunities.

## Recommendation Language

Recommendations should say "topics worth discussing with your professional team" rather than direct legal, tax, or investment advice.

Avoid:

- "You should invest in..."
- "You need this tax strategy..."
- "Change your estate plan..."
- "Buy this insurance..."

Prefer:

- "A topic worth discussing with your professional team..."
- "Consider reviewing..."
- "Your dashboard suggests this may be an area for deeper analysis..."

## Confidence Labels

Because Assessment v1 is intentionally lightweight, scores should carry diagnostic confidence labels.

Recommended labels:

- Initial Diagnostic
- Needs Financial Analysis
- Professional Team Discussion Recommended

## Future Scoring Enhancements

Future versions may include:

- User-type weighting for business owners, doctors, dentists, real estate investors, executives, retirees, and affluent families
- Financial Analysis inputs
- Advisor-reviewed score adjustments
- Household member perspectives
- Historical score trends
- Account and document data
- Opportunity scoring
